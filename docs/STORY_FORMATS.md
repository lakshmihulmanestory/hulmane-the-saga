# MahaHulmane — Story Formats Reference

> Quick reference for writers, artists, and AI agents.
> Before creating ANY content, identify which format it belongs to.

---

## Format Quick-Pick Guide

**Who is the audience?**
- Age 3–7 → **Kids Story (5 min)**
- Age 8–14 → **State Story Book** or **Animation Series**
- Age 6–12 family → **Animation Movie**
- Age 10+ reader → **Graphic Novel / Magazine**
- Age 13+ general → **Feature Film**
- Age 16+ gamer → **RPG Game**
- Age 10+ casual gamer → **Mobile Game**

**What is the content purpose?**
- Cultural education + adventure = **State Story Book**
- Quick bedtime read = **Kids Story**
- Visual storytelling = **Graphic Novel**
- Immersive play = **RPG or Mobile Game**
- Mass entertainment = **Film or Animation**

## Format 1 — Feature Film

```
CODE: FILM
Audience:   13+, general, family
Rating:     PG-13 equivalent
Length:     135–165 min
Tone:       Epic, cinematic, emotionally complex
Violence:   Moderate — battle sequences, no gore
```

### Story Structure
```
ACT 1 (25 min)  The kingdom in peace. Introduce hero, animal bond, daily life.
                Hint at the darkness. Establish the villain's origin.
ACT 2 (80 min)  The conflict escalates. Hero loses something. Villain gains power.
                The dark mirror scene — hero and villain face their shared animal.
ACT 3 (40 min)  The convergence. Final confrontation. Sacrifice. Resolution.
                Tattoo grows. New equilibrium. Hint at larger world.
CREDITS         Post-credit: hint at the next kingdom's story.
```

### Writing Rules for FILM format
- Dialogue can be complex — characters can contradict themselves
- Show the villain's motivation in at least 2 scenes — they must be understandable
- Regional language: 15–20 words/phrases woven naturally into dialogue
- No exposition dumps — world-building through action and environment
- The animal bond must be shown visually, not explained

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Storyboard-quality scene illustrations, concept art
- `videos/`: Trailer cut, scene animatics
- `audio/`: Score themes, dialogue direction notes

## Format 2 — Animation Series

```
CODE: ANIM-SERIES
Audience:   8–16, family co-viewing
Rating:     TV-PG / TV-Y7
Length:     22 min per episode, 12–16 episodes per season
Tone:       Action-adventure, humour, heart
Violence:   Stylised — characters knocked back, no blood
```

### Episode Structure
```
COLD OPEN (2 min)   Action hook or character moment — no context needed
ACT 1 (8 min)       Establish the episode's problem, hero's approach
ACT 2 (8 min)       Things go wrong. Animal bond tested. Ally/mentor moment.
ACT 3 (3 min)       Resolution. Small growth. One-liner that lands.
TAG (1 min)         Funny or warm moment. Sometimes villain reaction.
```

### Season Arc Structure
- Episodes 1–3: Introduce the hero and kingdom
- Episodes 4–8: Escalating challenges, introduce villain properly
- Episodes 9–11: Darkest point — hero's worst failure
- Episode 12–13: Resolution of season arc + hook for next season
- Episode 14–16 (if full season): Epilogue stories + crossover setup

### Writing Rules for ANIM-SERIES format
- Each episode must work as a standalone — assume new viewers
- Humour: physical comedy + wordplay (no adult jokes)
- Villain appears in at least every 3rd episode
- Each episode features the animal bond in an active, creative way
- Regional references: food, festivals, landscape, music — normalise, don't explain

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Character model sheets (on-model reference), background designs
- `videos/`: Episode animatics, opening sequence concept
- `audio/`: Character voice direction, episode score sketches

## Format 3 — Animation Movie (Theatrical)

```
CODE: ANIM-MOVIE
Audience:   6–12, kids + family
Rating:     U / G
Length:     85–95 min
Tone:       Heroic, warm, colourful
Violence:   Mild peril only — no real danger ever feels permanent
```

### Story Structure
```
OPENING (10 min)    Establish the hero as a child/young person who DOESN'T
                    have their power yet. Something is missing in their life.
MIDPOINT (40 min)   The animal bond forms. New power, new problems.
                    The "villain" is introduced — threatening but not scary.
CLIMAX (30 min)     The power alone isn't enough. The hero must use something
                    else — kindness, cleverness, trust.
RESOLUTION (15 min) Everything good. Animal bond fully established.
                    Tattoo level 1 earned. Sets up the series.
```

### Writing Rules for ANIM-MOVIE format
- The villain cannot be evil — they must be misguided, scared, or deceived
- The animal companion must have personality — comic moments + emotional moments
- Every 10 minutes: either a laugh or a "wow" moment
- No character deaths — loss must be non-permanent (friendship broken, object lost)
- The moral lesson must be speakable in one sentence by a 7-year-old

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Hero model (kid-friendly proportion — larger head, rounder features)
- `videos/`: Mood board video, colour script
- `audio/`: 3 key songs (opening theme, hero song, resolution reprise)

## Format 4A — Comics (Monthly / Serialised)

```
CODE: COMIC
Audience:   8–14 primary, readable by adults
Rating:     PG / All Ages
Length:     24–32 pages per issue. 3–6 issues = one complete arc.
Tone:       Bold, kinetic, action-first — Raj Comics meets modern superhero
Violence:   Stylised — impact panels, dust clouds, no blood
```

### Issue Structure
```
COVER          Hero or villain in dynamic action pose. Kingdom colour scheme.
               Issue number + arc name + state name.

PAGE 1–3       Hook: drop straight into action or a tense moment.
               No recap needed for issue 1 of an arc.
               Issues 2+: half-page recap panel at top of page 1.

PAGES 4–20     The story. 4–6 panels per page standard.
               Every 5 pages: one full-page splash or double-page spread.
               The animal bond must appear at least once.

PAGES 21–28    Climax and resolution — or cliff-hanger.
               If cliff-hanger: last panel must have a single bold line of dialogue.

PAGE 29–30     "KNOW YOUR KINGDOM" — back matter
               One cultural fact about the state.
               One real fact about the sacred animal.
               "Next issue:" teaser with thumbnail.

BACK COVER     Villain profile OR supporting character spotlight.
```

### Panel & Visual Rules for COMIC format
- **Speed lines** on every major action panel — convey motion even in still image
- **Impact frames**: white border flash when a hit lands
- **Tattoo visible** in every full-body hero panel — it is their identity
- **Animal gets its own panel** at least once per issue — never just background
- **Colour**: bold flat fills with one highlight. No gradients in action panels.
- **Typography**: sound effects hand-lettered style, large and integrated into panel art
- **Panel border**: carved stone motif for hero panels, cracked earth for villain panels

### Writing Rules for COMIC format
- Max 25 words per speech bubble — comics breathe through silence
- Each issue must work for a new reader AND reward returning readers
- Villain appears (or is referenced) in every issue
- Cliff-hanger or unresolved question at end of every issue
- One moment per issue that works without ANY dialogue — pure visual storytelling
- Regional language: 2–4 words/phrases per issue, explained in footnote at bottom of panel

### Arc Structure (3–6 issues)
```
Issue 1:  Establish the status quo. Introduce the threat. End: everything changes.
Issue 2:  Hero responds. First attempt fails. Villain gains advantage.
Issue 3:  Darkest point. Hero doubts themselves. Animal bond tested.
Issue 4:  New approach. Allies appear. The plan forms.
Issue 5:  Confrontation begins. Stakes maximised.
Issue 6:  Resolution. Tattoo grows. New equilibrium. Hint at next arc.
```
*(3-issue arcs compress: issues 1+2 = issues 1–2 above, 3 = 3+4+5, then 4 = 6)*

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Cover art, splash pages, character model sheets for comic style
- `samples/`: Panel layout references, Indian comics visual style references
- `audio/`: Digital edition — ambient sounds per panel (optional future feature)

## Format 4B — Graphic Novel / Magazine

```
CODE: GN
Audience:   10+, readers and collectors
Rating:     Teen
Length:     48–64 pages per issue, 200–250 pages per collected volume
Tone:       Stylised literary — Amar Chitra Katha meets Indian manga
Violence:   Moderate — shown in panels, impact frames, no explicit gore
```

### Page Structure
```
COVER PAGE      Full bleed hero + villain mirrored image. Kingdom name in Sanskrit.
CHAPTER BREAK   Full-page illustration with chapter title in decorative border
PANEL LAYOUT    3–5 panels per page standard. Full-page splash for key moments.
DIALOGUE        Kept short — max 30 words per speech bubble
CAPTION BOXES   Used for internal monologue and time/place markers
```

### Visual Language Rules
- Panel borders: carved stone / mandala motif (hero side) vs. cracked earth / serpent (villain side)
- Colour palette: 5–6 colours per issue, consistent with kingdom's regional colour identity
- The animal always gets at least one full-page spread per issue
- Tattoo must be visible in every full-body hero panel
- Cultural details in backgrounds: architecture, clothing, food, tools — researched and accurate

### Writing Rules for GN format
- Can handle subplot complexity — 2–3 story threads per issue
- Internal monologue shows what the hero doesn't say aloud
- The villain gets their own chapter (from their POV) in every collected volume
- Cliffhanger required at the end of every issue

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Panel art, cover illustrations, character turnarounds
- `samples/`: Visual style references, colour palette boards
- `audio/`: N/A (but QR-linked audio in digital edition)

## Format 5 — 5-Minute Kids Stories

```
CODE: KIDS-5MIN
Audience:   3–7, read-aloud or early reader
Rating:     U / All ages
Length:     500–800 words, 8–12 illustrations
Tone:       Warm, simple, celebratory, gently exciting
Violence:   NONE — challenges are natural (weather, being lost, something broken)
```

### Rigid Story Template (Do Not Deviate)
```
TITLE:     "[Hero Name] and the [Animal] Who [Did Something]"
           Example: "Tungabhadra and the Elephant Who Remembered"

OPENING (100 words):
  "In the kingdom of [State], where [distinctive landscape with 1 sensory detail],
   lived a hero named [Name]. Their best friend was [Animal Name],
   a [animal] who could [one special ability in simple words]."

THE PROBLEM (150 words):
  Something simple goes wrong. NO villain. Challenge = nature or circumstance.
  [Name] and [Animal] must work together.
  The problem must be relatable to a 5-year-old.

THE ANIMAL'S GIFT (150 words):
  The animal's ability helps. BUT — the hero must also do something:
  be brave, be patient, be kind, ask for help, keep trying.
  The animal cannot solve it alone.

THE RESOLUTION (100 words):
  Problem solved. The village/community celebrates.
  [Name]'s tattoo glows a little brighter.
  End with a warm image — a hug, a shared meal, a sunset.

THE LESSON (50 words, in italics):
  "And that day, [Name] learned that [simple moral truth]."
  One sentence. Never preachy. Examples:
  - "true courage is being scared and going anyway"
  - "listening is the greatest strength"
  - "home is wherever the people you love are"

ANIMAL FACT BOX (50 words):
  "Did you know? The [real animal name] is [one amazing real fact].
   In [State], this animal is [cultural significance in one sentence]."
```

### Illustration Rules for KIDS-5MIN
- Maximum 5 flat colours per illustration
- Hero always smiling or showing clear emotion (no ambiguous faces)
- Animal always friendly-looking, large eyes
- Backgrounds simple — 2–3 elements maximum
- Typography: large, rounded font, minimum 16pt equivalent

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Picture book illustrations (simple, flat, bold)
- `audio/`: Read-aloud audio recording (narrator + ambient sound)
- `videos/`: Animated read-along version (pan/zoom on illustrations)

## Format 6 — State Story Book Collection

```
CODE: STATE-BOOK
Audience:   8–14, cultural education + adventure
Rating:     All ages / Middle Grade
Length:     80–120 pages per book (28 books total = collectible set)
Tone:       Rich, culturally specific, adventure-forward
Violence:   Mild — described but not graphic; consequences matter
```

### Book Structure (Fixed)
```
FRONT MATTER
  • State map with key story locations marked
  • Hero profile card (name, age, animal, tattoo level, weapon, deity inspiration, colours)
  • Villain profile card (same format, dark colour scheme)
  • "Before you read..." — cultural context in 100 words

CHAPTER 1: THE KINGDOM (10 pages)
  Cultural immersion — geography, people, traditions, language, food.
  Written as if the reader is arriving in the kingdom for the first time.
  Must include: one local festival, one regional food, one architectural landmark.

CHAPTER 2: THE BOND (12 pages)
  How the hero first met their animal. The origin of the connection.
  Must show the animal acting on its own — it chose the hero, not just the reverse.
  Include: one real fact about the animal woven naturally into the story.

CHAPTER 3: THE FIRST TRIAL (15 pages)
  Hero earns tattoo levels 1 and 2.
  The challenge is local — something the people of that state would face.
  A mentor or elder figure must appear.

CHAPTER 4: THE MAIN ARC (40 pages)
  The primary story of this kingdom. Full hero journey.
  The villain appears and is given real motivation.
  At least one scene from the villain's POV.
  The animal bond is tested — the hero must fight without it, then reconnect.

CHAPTER 5: THE DARK MIRROR (10 pages)
  The villain's backstory told as a parallel chapter.
  Same animal. Same starting point. Different choice.
  The reader should feel sympathy for the villain, not just fear.

CHAPTER 6: THE RESOLUTION (10 pages)
  Resolution — not necessarily full victory.
  The hero earns tattoo level 3 or 4.
  Sets up the larger MahaHulmane world — a reference to another kingdom.

BACK MATTER
  • Cultural notes: real history behind the story's setting (2 pages)
  • Regional recipe or craft from the state (1 page)
  • Glossary of local language words used in the book
  • "Collect all 28 kingdoms" map with checkboxes
  • QR code: digital extras (audio, gallery, game code)
```

### Writing Rules for STATE-BOOK format
- Every cultural detail must be researched — no stereotypes
- The regional language must be woven in naturally, not as decoration
- The animal must be researched — include 2–3 real behaviours
- The villain must have at least one scene where you agree with them
- Each book must be readable without having read any other book in the series

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Chapter header illustrations, hero + villain profile art, map
- `samples/`: Style reference for illustrations, cultural research images
- `audio/`: Audiobook recording

## Format 7 — RPG Game (Big)

```
CODE: RPG
Audience:   16+, core gamers
Rating:     M (Mature)
Length:     60–100 hr main story, 200+ hr total
Tone:       Dark fantasy, morally complex, high stakes
Violence:   Full — combat, consequences shown
```

### Game World Structure
```
OPEN WORLD      All 28 kingdoms explorable from the start
MAIN QUEST      The Void Maw threat — requires uniting all 28 kingdoms
STATE QUESTS    Each kingdom = 8–12 hours of story content
SIDE QUESTS     Village-level stories, animal companion quests, historical mysteries
TATTOO SYSTEM   = Skill tree. Each tattoo stage unlocks new abilities.
ANIMAL BOND     = Active companion. Gives gameplay hints, story dialogue, abilities.
ALIGNMENT       Some quest branches offer hero OR villain path choices.
```

### Lore Writing Rules for RPG format
- Every NPC has a name and at least one line that reveals their personal story
- Environmental storytelling: inscriptions, murals, broken objects tell history
- The villain faction has recruitable members — not all are evil
- Regional culture deeply embedded: music changes by kingdom, food vendors, festivals
- The animal bond degrades if the player makes choices against the animal's nature

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Concept art, environment art, character models
- `audio/`: Full orchestral score (28 kingdom themes + combat + ambient)
- `videos/`: Cutscene animatics, trailer

## Format 8 — Mobile Game

```
CODE: MOBILE
Audience:   10+, casual to mid-core
Rating:     E10+ / PEGI 7
Length:     Session-based, ongoing live service
Tone:       Colourful, competitive, accessible
Violence:   Stylised — characters flash and fall, no blood or consequences
```

### Game Structure
```
HEROES          All 28 heroes (+ variants) as collectible characters
UPGRADE PATH    Tattoo system = 4-tier upgrade (footprint → full body)
ABILITIES       Each hero: 3 abilities tied to their animal bond
TEAMS           Regional bonuses when fielding heroes from same region
MODES           Story mode (narrative), Arena (PvP), Kingdom defense (co-op)
EVENTS          Seasonal: festivals, new story content, crossover characters
```

### Content Rules for MOBILE format
- Characters must be recognisable at 64×64 pixel icon size — strong silhouette
- No pay-to-win — cosmetics and speed only
- Every hero unlockable through gameplay
- Villain characters available as alternate skins for heroes (same animal, dark palette)

### Media Needed
- `images/`: Character icons (64×64, 128×128, full art), UI elements
- `audio/`: Short character voice lines (4–6 per hero), ability sounds, kingdom themes
- `videos/`: Character reveal trailers (30 sec each)

#### 9A — Stickers

```
CODE: STICKER
Sizes:        5cm, 8cm, custom sheet (6–12 per sheet)
Finish:       Matte, gloss, holographic (holographic = hero's aura/tattoo areas)
Quantity:     One sticker per hero + per villain + per animal = 84 base designs
```

**Art Rules for STICKERS:**
- **Silhouette must read clearly at 5cm** — if you can't tell who it is from the outline alone, redesign
- White or transparent border around character — 2mm minimum
- No text on the sticker itself (text on packaging only)
- Tattoo must be visible and clear — it is the hero's most recognisable visual element
- Hero sticker: kingdom's primary colour as background or aura
- Villain sticker: same pose/animal as hero but inverted/dark palette — they are a pair
- Animal sticker: the animal alone, sacred glow treatment, no character
- **Holographic treatment**: hero stickers get holographic sheen on the tattoo area only

**Sticker Sets (collections to sell together):**
- Per state: 1 hero + 1 villain + 1 animal = 3-sticker kingdom set
- Per region: all states in region = regional mega-set
- Sacred Animals: all 28 animals only = wildlife education set
- Tattoo Progression: one hero at all 4 tattoo levels = growth set

**Media Needed:**
- `images/`: Sticker-ready art at 300 DPI, transparent background, clean outline
- File format: PNG (transparent) + PDF (print-ready with bleed)

#### 9B — Collector Cards

```
CODE: CARD
Size:         Standard trading card (63mm × 88mm) + tarot size (70mm × 120mm)
Finish:       Premium card stock, foil on key details
Sets:         Hero cards, Villain cards, Animal cards, Story scene cards
```

**Card Layout — Hero Card:**
```
FRONT
  Top 60%:    Character illustration (portrait, facing slightly left or right)
  Bottom 40%: Dark gradient panel
              Hero name (bold, large)
              Title / epithet (smaller, italic)
              State name + region icon
              Animal symbol (small, top-right corner)
              Tattoo level indicator (4 dots — filled = earned)

BACK
  Background: Kingdom colour gradient + sacred animal watermark
  Stats block:
    POWER    ████████░░  8/10
    SPEED    ██████░░░░  6/10
    WISDOM   █████████░  9/10
    BOND     ██████████  10/10  ← the animal bond score
  Fighting Style: one line
  Weapon: one line
  Deity Inspiration: one line
  QR code → links to full character page on website
```

**Card Layout — Animal Card:**
```
FRONT
  Full bleed realistic animal illustration (no character, just the animal)
  Sacred glow treatment — the animal has divine presence
  Animal name (Sanskrit + English)
  State name

BACK
  3 real facts about this animal
  Conservation status
  Cultural significance in this state
  "Bonded with: [Hero name] (good spirit) / [Villain name] (dark spirit)"
```

**Card Rarities:**
- Common: All 28 heroes (base art)
- Uncommon: All 28 villains
- Rare: All 28 animals (detailed realistic art)
- Very Rare: Hero at full-body tattoo (glowing artwork)
- Ultra Rare: Hero + Villain dual card (one card, split down the middle)
- Secret Rare: The Grand Council (7-card set that tiles together into one scene)

**Media Needed:**
- `images/`: Card art at 300 DPI, exact card dimensions + bleed
- `samples/`: Reference for card layout style, Indian trading card precedents

#### 9C — T-Shirts & Apparel

```
CODE: APPAREL
Products:     T-shirts, hoodies, caps, socks, tote bags
Audience:     Kids (3–12) + Adult (18+) separate lines
Print method: Screen print (bold designs) + DTG (detailed art)
```

**Design Categories:**

| Design Type | Description | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Kingdom Badge | State emblem + animal + kingdom name. Clean, circular, badge format | All ages |
| Hero Silhouette | Pure silhouette of hero in action pose. Kingdom colour + tattoo outline | Adults |
| Animal Portrait | Realistic animal art, no character. "Sacred Animal of [State]" text | All ages |
| Tattoo Art | The sacred tattoo design extracted and enlarged. Runs down sleeve or full back | Adults |
| Duo Art | Hero + Villain facing each other, split colour. No text needed | Adults |
| Chibi / Cute | Simplified, round-featured heroes for kids' line | Kids |
| Typography | "MAHAHULMANE" + regional language + Sanskrit in strong typographic layout | Adults |

**Art Rules for APPAREL:**
- Designs must work on dark AND light fabric — test both
- No more than 4 colours on screen print designs
- The animal must be the largest element on animal-focused designs
- "Kingdom Badge" designs: always include the state name in the regional language script
- For kids' line: always bright, always the hero wins, villain only as shadow reference

**Media Needed:**
- `images/`: Print-ready vector files (AI or SVG), shown on mockup
- File format: SVG (vector) for screen print, PNG 300 DPI for DTG

#### 9D — Art Prints & Posters

```
CODE: PRINT
Sizes:        A4, A3, A2, A1, 18"×24" (poster), 24"×36" (large)
Finish:       Matte fine art, gloss poster, risograph (limited editions)
Edition type: Open edition (standard) + Limited edition (numbered, signed)
```

**Print Categories:**

| Series | Description |
|---|---|
| Kingdom Portraits | One hero + villain per state, side-by-side. Temple/landscape background. |
| Sacred Animals | Realistic animal in natural habitat, sacred glow. No characters. |
| The Divine Echo | Scene prints — the final confrontation moment of each state's story |
| The Tattoo Progression | One hero shown at all 4 tattoo levels. Collectible 4-print set per hero. |
| Regional Panorama | All heroes from one region together in an epic landscape |
| The Grand Council | All 7 council members in one large-format composition |
| State Map Print | Illustrated map of the state with story locations, landmarks, characters |

**Art Rules for PRINTS:**
- Minimum resolution: 300 DPI at print size (A2 = at least 4961 × 7016 px)
- Kingdom Portrait prints: the split-light composition (divine white left / dark red right)
- Sacred Animal prints: must be usable as standalone wildlife art — beautiful without knowing the story
- State Map prints: must include real geographical features + story landmarks + hero/villain symbols

**Limited Edition Rules:**
- Numbered (e.g. "47 of 200")
- Each limited print has one unique hidden detail not in the open edition
- QR code on the back — authenticates and links to the story

**Media Needed:**
- `images/`: High-res print-ready art, minimum 300 DPI at final print size
- File format: TIFF or PDF/X for print production

#### 9E — Toys & Collectible Figures

```
CODE: TOY
Products:     Action figures, vinyl figures, plush animals, puzzle sets
Audience:     Figures: 6+ / Plush: 3+ / Collector vinyl: 14+
Scale:        Action figures: 1:12 (15cm) / Vinyl: 10cm / Plush: 20–30cm
```

**Product Lines:**

**Action Figures (15cm, articulated)**
- Hero figure: 12 points of articulation, tattoo detail sculpted in
- Comes with: traditional weapon + modern weapon (both included)
- Accessory: small animal companion figure (3–5cm)
- Packaging: kingdom-themed box, state map on back, story summary
- Collect: hero + villain sold separately — designed to face off on a shelf

**Vinyl Collector Figures (10cm, limited edition)**
- Artist-designed, non-articulated
- Bold flat colour palette matching the kingdom's colours
- Each figure: variant "dark" version available (villain palette swap)
- Limited to 500–1000 per design
- Trophy-style base engraved with kingdom name in Sanskrit

**Plush Animals (20–30cm)**
- The sacred animal ONLY — no human characters
- Realistic but huggable proportions
- Sacred marking embroidered (the tilak / sacred pattern specific to that animal)
- Hangtag: "I am the [animal] of [State]. My hero is [Name]." + real animal fact
- These must work as standalone wildlife plush — no franchise markings needed on the animal itself

**Puzzle Sets**
- 500 piece: Single state scene (hero + villain + animal in their environment)
- 1000 piece: Regional panorama (all heroes from one region)
- 2000 piece: The Grand Council scene
- Back of box: State story summary + character guide

**Art Rules for TOYS:**
- The tattoo must be sculpted/printed on figures — it is non-negotiable identity
- Hero and villain figures must be clearly distinguishable by silhouette alone
- Plush animals: the sacred marking (tilak, forehead mark) must be present
- All toy packaging must include one educational fact about the real animal

**Media Needed:**
- `images/`: Turnaround sheets (front/back/side/detail), packaging mockups
- `samples/`: Reference for Indian toy aesthetics, existing product comparisons
