## The Core Idea

Hulmane-The-Saga is an original Indian fantasy universe where **every Indian state is a kingdom**
with its own mythology, sacred animal, hero, and villain. The world draws from the Mahabharata,
Hindu gods, and India's rich regional cultures — but tells entirely new stories.

The franchise is built to work across **every storytelling medium** — from a 5-minute picture
book read to a 5-year open-world RPG. The same world, the same characters, scaled to fit.

## The World Rules (Lore Bible Summary)

### The Tattoo System
Every hero and villain carries a **sacred beast tattoo** that grows as they earn power:

| Level | Mark | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Footprint / Pawprint | First act of heroism — village or community scale |
| 2 | Foreleg | Kingdom-level recognition |
| 3 | Half Body | Regional champion status |
| 4 | Full Body | State leader — seat at the Grand Council |

The tattoo is the visual shorthand the audience uses to track who is powerful.
**Never depict a character above their earned tattoo level.**

### The Animal Bond
- Each kingdom's hero is **bonded to the state's sacred animal**
- The bond grants a power tied to that animal's nature (elephant → seismic memory, lion → roar of authority, etc.)
- The villain shares the **same animal** but the bond is corrupted — the animal's strength turned destructive
- Hero and villain are **mirrors**, not simple good vs. evil. Both have real motivations.

### The Deity Mapping
Every hero is inspired by a specific Hindu deity or Mahabharata figure.
This gives the character their **fighting style, personality archetype, and visual signature**.
See `docs/CHARACTERS.md` for the full 28-hero deity map.

### The Power Hierarchy
```
HULMANE-THE-SAGA Grand Council   (top — 7 leaders)
      ↑
6 Regional Leaders          (one per region)
      ↑
28 State Heroes             (one per kingdom)
      ↑
Village / Clan champions    (entry level — where most stories start)
```

### The Threat
Beyond internal kingdom politics, an external cosmic threat — **The Void Maw** — approaches.
Only a united Hulmane-The-Saga can withstand it. This is the overarching arc that ties all 28 kingdoms.

## The Transmedia Plan

Hulmane-The-Saga is designed from the ground up as a **transmedia franchise**.
Each format tells different parts of the same world — they do not duplicate each other.

### Format 1 — Feature Film Series
- **Scope:** One film per major story arc (2–3 films per region)
- **Audience:** 13+ general, PG-13 equivalent
- **Tone:** Epic, cinematic, emotionally complex — think Baahubali meets Mahabharata
- **Length:** 2 hours 15 min – 2 hours 45 min
- **Story structure:** Hero's journey with ensemble cast, political intrigue, battle sequences
- **First film:** Karnataka arc (The Buried Temple / Vijayanagara Reborn)
- **Visual style:** Dark fantasy realism, Vijayanagara-era architecture as aesthetic base
- **Content note:** Violence (non-graphic), moral complexity, no explicit content

### Format 2 — Animation Series (Streaming)
- **Scope:** One series per region (6 regions = 6 seasons), each season 12–16 episodes
- **Audience:** 8–16, family co-viewing
- **Tone:** Action-adventure, humour, heart — think Avatar: The Last Airbender
- **Length:** 22 minutes per episode
- **Story structure:** Episodic with season arc — each episode is self-contained but builds
- **Visual style:** Stylised, vibrant Indian colour palette, anime-influenced fluidity
- **Content note:** Action but no gore, age-appropriate villains, strong moral themes
- **Music:** Original Indian classical fusion score

### Format 3 — Animation Movie (Theatrical)
- **Scope:** One film per major hero — introduces a hero to a younger audience
- **Audience:** 6–12, kids + family
- **Tone:** Heroic, colourful, emotionally warm — think Pixar/DreamWorks with Indian soul
- **Length:** 85–95 minutes
- **Story structure:** Classic hero journey, clear villain, resolution with growth
- **Visual style:** Bright, expressive, accessible — less realistic than the film series
- **Content note:** Mild peril, no violence, positive role models
- **Key difference from series:** Each movie is a standalone origin story

### Format 4A — Comics (Monthly / Serialised)
- **Scope:** Monthly serialised comics — shorter, punchier, cliff-hanger driven
- **Audience:** 8–14 primary, 10+ crossover with adult readers
- **Tone:** Bold, kinetic, action-first — Raj Comics + Tinkle + modern superhero style
- **Length:** 24–32 pages per issue. 3–6 issues = one story arc.
- **Story structure:** Single issue = one chapter. Each issue ends on a cliff-hanger.
- **Visual style:** Dynamic panel layouts, speed lines, impact frames — built for action
- **Content note:** Age-appropriate, heroes always survive, mild peril
- **Publication plan:** Weekly digital + monthly print. Karnataka launches first.
- **Special feature:** Each issue has a "Know Your Kingdom" back page — one cultural fact + animal fact

### Format 4B — Graphic Novel / Magazine Series
- **Scope:** Collected comics arcs + prestige standalone stories per state
- **Audience:** 10+, readers/collectors
- **Tone:** Stylised, literary — Amar Chitra Katha meets modern Indian manga
- **Length:** 48–64 pages per issue, 200–250 pages per collected volume
- **Story structure:** Manga-style chapter breaks, visual storytelling priority
- **Visual style:** Bold linework, flat Indian colour palettes, temple motif panel borders
- **Content note:** Teen-appropriate, can handle complex themes in visual form
- **Publication plan:** One state per quarter (7 years for all 28, then crossovers)

### Format 5 — 5-Minute Stories for Kids
- **Scope:** One story per hero (28 initial), then supporting characters and animals
- **Audience:** 3–7, read-aloud by parents / early readers
- **Tone:** Warm, simple, moral lesson, celebratory of Indian culture
- **Length:** 500–800 words per story, 8–12 illustrations
- **Story structure:** Problem → Hero uses their animal's gift → Resolution → Lesson
- **Visual style:** Bold, simple, picture-book illustrations — maximum 4–5 colours
- **Content note:** Fully child-safe, animals are friendly companions, no villains (shadows only)
- **Example story:** "Tungabhadra and the Elephant Who Remembered" (Karnataka, 5 min)
- **Distribution:** App, physical book, school reading programmes

### Format 6 — State Story Book Collection (Collectible Series)
- **Scope:** One book per state = 28 books total, sold as a collectible set
- **Audience:** 8–14, cultural education + adventure reading
- **Tone:** Rich storytelling, cultural pride, regional specificity — this is the deep version
- **Length:** 80–120 pages per book
- **Story structure:** Full hero origin + main story arc + cultural notes + recipe/craft from the region
- **Visual style:** Full-colour illustrated novel — detailed art every 2–3 pages
- **Content note:** Teen-appropriate, references real history and geography
- **Special features:** QR codes linking to the app, state map inside cover, glossary of regional terms
- **Collection angle:** "Collect all 28 — one for every kingdom of Bharata"

### Format 7 — Big RPG Game (AAA / Console)
- **Scope:** Open-world action RPG — the full Hulmane-The-Saga universe as a game world
- **Audience:** 16+, core gamers
- **Tone:** Dark fantasy, morally complex — the adult version of the world
- **Length:** 60–100 hour main story + 200+ hours of side content
- **Gameplay:** Explore all 28 kingdoms, complete tattoo trials, build alliances, face the Void Maw
- **Key mechanic:** The tattoo system = skill tree. Your tattoo level determines your abilities.
- **Key mechanic:** Animal bond = companion AI that gives powers and story hints
- **Key mechanic:** Hero vs. Villain choice — you can play either side in some arcs
- **Visual style:** Realistic dark fantasy, Unreal Engine 5 fidelity
- **Content note:** M-rated — violence, complex moral themes, mature storytelling

### Format 8 — Mobile Game (Mid-Core)
- **Scope:** Strategic action game — build your kingdom's hero team
- **Audience:** 10+, casual to mid-core mobile gamers
- **Tone:** Colourful, accessible, competitive
- **Gameplay:** Collect heroes (all 28 + variants), form teams, battle in arena and story modes
- **Key mechanic:** Tattoo system = upgrade path (footprint → full body = 4 upgrade tiers)
- **Key mechanic:** Animal spirit abilities = special moves
- **Key mechanic:** Regional bonuses — south heroes stronger together, etc.
- **Visual style:** Bold, stylised — similar to the animation series
- **Monetisation model:** Cosmetic only, no pay-to-win

## The State Story Books — Detailed Spec

Each of the 28 state books follows this structure:

```
FRONT MATTER
  - Map of the kingdom with key locations
  - Hero profile card (name, animal, tattoo level, weapon, deity inspiration)
  - Villain profile card (same format, dark colour scheme)

CHAPTERS
  Chapter 1: The Kingdom (cultural introduction — geography, people, traditions)
  Chapter 2: The Birth of the Bond (origin — how the hero first met their animal)
  Chapter 3: The First Trial (earning tattoo level 1–2)
  Chapter 4: The Main Story Arc (the primary conflict of this kingdom's story)
  Chapter 5: The Dark Mirror (the villain's perspective — why they chose their path)
  Chapter 6: The Resolution (how the story ends — open enough for sequels)

BACK MATTER
  - Cultural notes: real history behind the story's setting
  - Regional recipe or craft from the state
  - Glossary of local language words used in the book
  - "Find all 28 kingdoms" collector's map
  - QR code to digital extras
```

## The 5-Minute Kids Stories — Detailed Spec

Each kids story follows this rigid structure (important for consistency across 28+ stories):

```
OPENING (100 words)
  "In the kingdom of [State], where [distinctive landscape],
   lived a hero named [Name] and their friend, [Animal]."

THE PROBLEM (150 words)
  Something simple goes wrong — a flood, a lost child, a fire, crops failing.
  The villain is NOT introduced. The challenge is nature or circumstance.

THE ANIMAL'S GIFT (150 words)
  The animal's special ability helps solve the problem.
  The hero learns something about patience / courage / kindness.

THE RESOLUTION (100 words)
  Problem solved. Village celebrates.
  The hero's tattoo grows one mark.

THE LESSON (50 words, italicised)
  One clear sentence about the value demonstrated.
  Example: "True strength is knowing when to ask for help."

ANIMAL FACT (50 words)
  One real fact about the actual state animal.
  Example: "The Indian Elephant can remember paths walked 50 years ago."
```
