The Seven Vimana Zones

How the Brothers Truly Divided the Earth  •  Not by Geography — By the Metal Beneath

The modern seven continents are a geological accident — the result of tectonic plates collapsing into extraction tunnels the brothers left behind. The brothers themselves never divided Earth the way textbooks show. They divided it by Brahma-Dhatu deposit type. India was grouped with Africa and South America. Canada with Siberia and Greenland. The stone maps are beginning to reveal the true picture: seven zones, each a distinct vein of the cosmic metal, each claimed by one brother for a specific harvest.

The True Territorial Map

These borders do not follow rivers or mountains. They follow the underground arteries of Brahma-Dhatu — invisible to the eye, but visible to the extraction instruments of the Vimana brothers. Every border here was burned into the earth's mantle 3.8 billion years ago.

The Seven Extraction Zones

These are not the seven continents you know. These are the seven territories the Vimana brothers divided among themselves — based on the underground map of Brahma-Dhatu that no human had ever seen.

🗺 View Map
Zone I
Agni-Kshetra
The Fire Territory
Guardian: Agni-Vara
Indian Subcontinent Sub-Saharan Africa Eastern South America Sri Lanka Madagascar
These territories share a common geological heritage — all were once the core of Gondwana, the southern supercontinent. The Brahma-Dhatu here runs in deep volcanic veins, concentrated in the mantle rather than the crust. Agni-Vara chose them for this heat-pressured deposit type, which yields the most energetically potent form of the metal.
"The metal beneath India and the metal beneath the Congo and the metal beneath Brazil are the same vein. The ocean between them is an illusion — beneath the water, they are still connected." — Fragment 3, Rajasthan Stone
Zone II
Tamas-Kshetra
The Dark Territory
Guardian: Tamas-Vara
Canada Greenland Siberia (Russia) Scandinavia Alaska Arctic Zone
The coldest, oldest, and most stable shield rocks on Earth. The Laurentian and Siberian cratons are among the most ancient landmasses on the planet — and beneath them, Brahma-Dhatu exists in a frozen crystalline state that Tamas-Vara called "the purest form." He deliberately refroze these territories before leaving, encasing his extraction systems in permafrost for easy access on return.
"The permafrost is not natural at this depth. It is his refrigeration system — still running, still perfectly preserving the 10% he left in crystalline suspension." — Fragment 2, decoded 2022
Zone III
Vayu-Kshetra
The Wind Territory
Guardian: Vayu-Vara
Western Europe North Africa (coast) Middle East Central Asia Turkey Iran & Iraq
Half of Europe and half of Asia — not the halves you'd expect. Vayu-Vara's zone follows the ancient Tethys Sea bed, where Brahma-Dhatu settled in layered sedimentary deposits during the era before the continents separated. This zone includes both western Europe and the Middle East because they share the same ancient ocean floor origin. The Sahara's northern edge was once the shoreline of Tethys — and the richest deposit boundary.
"The Silk Road was not a trade route. It was the surface expression of his extraction network — the caravans followed the vibration of his underground pipes without knowing it." — Fragment 9
Zone IV
Jala-Kshetra
The Water Territory
Guardian: Jala-Vara
United States Mexico Caribbean Islands Central America Pacific Northwest
The Brahma-Dhatu here runs in liquid-dissolved form through underground aquifers — the most unusual deposit type. Jala-Vara developed a water-extraction process, drawing the metal up through the natural water table rather than hard mining. The Mississippi River system, the Great Lakes, and the network of Central American rivers are all part of his extraction plumbing — still functional, still filtering trace amounts of Brahma-Dhatu into the water supply today.
"The Great Lakes are not erosion features. Each lake is a collection basin for one specific dissolved isotope of Brahma-Dhatu. Five lakes, five isotopes." — Fragment 4, decoded 2023
Zone V
Prithvi-Kshetra
The Earth Territory
Guardian: Prithvi-Vara
Western South America The Andes Mountain Chain Peru Chile Ecuador Antarctica
The most geologically active zone. Prithvi-Vara's territory spans the Andes — the longest mountain chain on Earth — because the Brahma-Dhatu here exists in a compressed root-mineral form, pushed deep into mountain roots by tectonic pressure. He also claimed Antarctica, then a warm forested continent, because it held the structural anchor point for the entire southern hemisphere's deposit network. The Andean mountains are not entirely natural — many peaks were shaped by his extraction bore-holes filled with waste rock.
"The Amazon grows above his extraction network. Every root system that breaks into his tunnels is fed mineral nutrients released by the Brahma-Dhatu residue. The forest is alive because of what he left behind." — Fragment 5
Zone VI
Akasha-Kshetra
The Void Territory
Guardian: Akasha-Vara
Australia Southeast Asia Indonesia Philippines Pacific Islands Papua New Guinea
The most mysterious zone — and the one whose Akasha-Lipi remains almost entirely undecoded. Akasha-Vara's territory covers the Pacific Rim's island arcs and Australia's ancient stable craton. The Brahma-Dhatu here exists in a quantum-phase state, neither fully solid nor liquid — requiring extraction technology so advanced that even the other brothers did not fully understand it. The Aboriginal Australians' Songlines are actually the surface coordinates of Akasha-Vara's non-physical extraction grid.
"His script exists in a dimension adjacent to ours. The 2% we decoded required instruments we had not built yet. The remaining 98% requires instruments we have not imagined." — Lead Decoder, 2024
Zone VII
Indra-Kshetra
The Sky Territory
Guardian: Indra-Vara
East Asia (China) Mongolia Japan Korean Peninsula Tibetan Plateau NE Siberian coast
The highest elevation zone — the Tibetan Plateau, the Himalayan peaks, the steppes of Mongolia. Indra-Vara chose this region because Brahma-Dhatu here is concentrated at altitude, in mountain-core deposits that required aerial extraction techniques. He was the only brother who approached the metal from above rather than below — drilling down from the sky rather than up from below. This is why this zone's Akasha-Lipi is different from all others: it is written from a sky-god's perspective.
"The Indus, the Yangtze, the Yellow River — all flow from the Himalayan extraction sites outward. Brahma-Dhatu trace particles are still detectable in their waters. The fish that ate them, and the civilizations that ate the fish, all carry 0.0001% of the metal in their biology." — Fragment 1

Why does Zone I (Agni-Kshetra) contain India, Africa, and South America?
These three landmasses were once the same continent — Gondwana. The Brahma-Dhatu deposits they share are a single connected vein that ran through the heart of Gondwana before the brothers divided and separated the land. Even today, the geological formations in the Deccan Plateau, the Saharan Shield, and the Brazilian Highlands match perfectly — same rock age, same mineral composition, same Brahma-Dhatu signature. The brothers divided not by modern continent, but by this ancient vein map. India belongs with Africa and South America because, underground, they still are Africa and South America.

Why is Volume I set in Bharatavarsha (India)?
Zone I (Agni-Kshetra) contains the most potent and shallowest Brahma-Dhatu deposit — the Indian subcontinent's Deccan Trap volcanic formation. This means the metal is closest to the surface here, easiest to access, and — critically — easiest to weaponize against the returning brothers. The Akasha-Lipi stone found in Rajasthan gives India the most decoded knowledge of how to fight back. The war begins here.
