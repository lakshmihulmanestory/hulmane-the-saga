Agni-Kshetra — Zone I

The Sacred Fire Realm  •  Gondwana's Ancient Heart

India, Africa, and Eastern South America are not three separate lands. Beneath the ocean, they are a single vein of Brahma-Dhatu — the most concentrated volcanic deposit on Earth. Agni-Vara claimed all three as one territory, one harvest, one fire.

🔥 Why One Zone?

India, Africa, and Eastern South America share the same underground Brahma-Dhatu vein — a remnant of Gondwana, the ancient supercontinent they once formed together. Agni-Vara did not see three landmasses. He saw one continuous deposit stretching beneath what would later become three oceans.

♦ The Deccan Plateau

The main Brahma-Dhatu deposit in Zone I lies beneath the Deccan Plateau — the volcanic shield of central India. This is the shallowest and most accessible deposit. It is why the Akasha-Lipi was found in Rajasthan, and why Volume I begins in Bharatavarsha. The war starts here.

🌿 The Living Library

The Amazon rainforest grows directly above Agni-Vara's extraction network. Mineral nutrients released by the Brahma-Dhatu residue feed the ecosystem. The forest's extraordinary biodiversity is not evolution — it is the unintended consequence of billions of years of a fire-being's underground work.

△ The Rift Connection

Africa's Great Rift Valley marks the seam where Gondwana first began to split — pulled apart by the stress of Agni-Vara's deep extraction network. The volcanoes, the rift lakes, and the seismic activity of East Africa are all sustained by the 10% of Brahma-Dhatu Agni-Vara left behind as structural support.
