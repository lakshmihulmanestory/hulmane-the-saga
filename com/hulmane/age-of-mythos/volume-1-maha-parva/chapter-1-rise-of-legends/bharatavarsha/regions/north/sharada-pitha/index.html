The Hero

Sharad-Pandit — The Pandit of the Stag

Born in a houseboat library on Dal Lake. Eighth of his line. Carries the Sharada-Patra scroll-shield diagonally across his back, ink-stained fingers, a quartz reading-lens at his belt.

Male North Hangul Stag

Sacred Animal

Hangul (Cervus hanglu hanglu) — the Kashmir Stag

Witness. Rarity. The survival of memory. Sharad-Pandit reads snow-tracks left years before — the stag’s seeing made instinct.

The Villain

Avantivarman-Bhrasta — The Erasing Historian

The chief librarian of Sharada-Pitha who reads each manuscript once, remembers it perfectly, then burns it. He believes he is curing the kingdom of its own confusion.

Kingdom Landscape

Vale of Kashmir — Pir Panjal range — Jhelum river — Dal Lake — saffron fields of Pampore — the Awantipur ruins — and beyond the high passes, the ancient Sharada Peeth temple itself. The cold preserves what fire would take.
