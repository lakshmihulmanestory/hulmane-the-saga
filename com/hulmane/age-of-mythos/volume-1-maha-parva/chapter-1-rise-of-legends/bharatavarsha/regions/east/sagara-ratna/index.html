The Hero

Onge-Nakshatra — The Navigator of the Salt-Stars

Born mid-storm in his mother’s outrigger. Half-Onge, half-Sagaravamsha by an island-shipwreck marriage. The islands’ first inter-tribal child. Constellation-paint at dawn, Sagara-Bandhu coiled at the hip.

Male East Saltwater Crocodile

Sacred Animal

Saltwater Crocodile (Crocodylus porosus)

Tidal patience. Ambush from below. The oldest predator of the islands — the witness that watches mainland ships arrive and never leave. Through its bond, Onge-Nakshatra reads tides like a page.

The Villain

Kalapani-Asura — The Warden of Forgotten Cells

Not one man — the accumulated memory-pain of every prisoner who died in the Cellular Jail of Port Blair, wearing the body of the last colonial warden who hanged himself in 1948. He locks any space he touches.

Kingdom Landscape

Bay of Bengal islands — Port Blair Cellular Jail — Havelock and Neil reefs — North Sentinel sanctuary — Nicobar mangroves — Indira Point lighthouse, southernmost tip of Bharatavarsha. The only Volume I kingdom that is not a continental landmass — the launch-point to the next continent.
