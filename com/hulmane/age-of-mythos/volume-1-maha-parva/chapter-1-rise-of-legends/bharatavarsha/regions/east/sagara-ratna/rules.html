The Sacred Tattoo — Onge-Nakshatra’s Mark

The tattoo is the saltwater crocodile rising from the surf — tail wrapping the upper arm, body spreading across the back as he reaches each island and weathers each storm. The constellation Mrigashira hovers above the crocodile’s eye in the final stage.

Stage IThe Surf Line
A wave-break across the shoulder-blade with a single crocodile-eye in its crest. Given the dawn after he was born — Aki tattooed it with a thorn while he nursed.
Stage IIThe Rising Tail
Tail wraps the upper arm. Earned at the Cellular Jail when he opens every cell at once and the ghosts walk out to the sea.
Stage IIIThe Half Reef
Crocodile body spread across upper back — reef-coral pattern at the spine. Earned when the inter-tribal council survives its first season.
Stage IVFull Surf — Bridge
Full crocodile across the back with Mrigashira above and Nicobar Pigeon in flight at the lower right. Earned when the first canoe crosses Indira Point and returns — the moment Bharatavarsha’s southern horizon opens for Volume II.

Sacred Weapons — The Sagara-Bandhu

Ancient Weapon
Sagara-Bandhu (The Ocean’s Bond)
Type
A fist-sized coral-stone anchor on a salt-cured kelp-fiber line — one cowrie shell inlaid in the stone’s eye.
Ability
When swung in a horizontal arc the anchor pulls water with it — creating a small typhoon-circle that disorients on land and drowns enemies caught in shallow surf. When struck against any locked thing — door, cell, sealed hold — the salt in the line eats the lock open within seconds.
Origin
Carved from a piece of bleached fire-coral that washed ashore on Little Andaman after the 2004 tsunami — the only piece large enough to hold its shape. Aki cured the kelp line over twelve seasons of salt and sun.
Modern Weapon
Lavana-Jaala — The Salt Net
Type
An underwater drone-net of biopolymer that lays itself across reefs as an invisible barrier.
Ability
Detects ship-hull movement and signals to a wrist-mounted display. The net dissolves harmlessly within a year and reforms from sea-water minerals when re-activated — defends without persisting. Cannot be detected by sonar.
How It Works
Calcium-and-magnesium nanostructures self-assemble in sea water under low-power inductive charge from buoy-mounted solar batteries. The same mineral cycle a coral reef uses — weaponised for early warning.

Kingdom Vehicles

Ancient
Aki-Nauka — The First Canoe
A double-outrigger canoe carved by Aki herself from a single mahua trunk fifty-three years ago — bound with hand-twisted coir rope — sails of pandanus mat. The hull remembers every storm it has crossed; the wood hums different notes on different currents. It will outlive its carver.
Modern
Sagara-Ratha — Hydrofoil Skiff
A solar-electric hydrofoil that rises clear of the water at speed — silent, leaves no wake, has no metal hull (composites only). Designed to slip past the colonial-era radar that the Andaman jail-network still uses to track movements. Can cross from Port Blair to Indira Point in under five hours.
