## 1. The Big Arc — Volume I in One Page

Chapter 1 — **Rise of Legends** (DONE). 28 kingdoms, each with its hero and its villain, established. Every hero earned tattoo level 1–2. Every villain survived. Each kingdom stands alone — no inter-kingdom war yet.

Chapter 2 — **Civil War**. The threat is no longer external. In every kingdom the *internal* fault line of Chapter 1 cracks open. The villains from Chapter 1 do not return as direct attackers — they return as **catalysts**, exploiting each hero's *defining flaw* to split the kingdom from within. 28 kingdoms × 28 distinct civil-war catalysts. Heroes lose ground. Some heroes fall. Villains seize regional power.

Chapter 3 — **Rise of Beasts**. The sacred animals themselves stir. Heroes who survived Chapter 2 reach tattoo level 3. Out of 28 kingdoms, **6 emerge as Regional Dominants** — one per region. The remaining 22 either fall, swear fealty, or are absorbed. The Grand Council (which until now was symbolic) becomes real: 6 seats, one per region. Each Regional Dominant carries the burden of the kingdoms beneath it.

Chapter 4 — **The Great Epic**. The 6 regional dominants now face each other. An external cosmic threat (the **Void Maw**, hinted across all earlier chapters) chooses this fracture to invade. Only one Regional Dominant can be the seventh seat — the **Maha-Adhipati** (Great Sovereign) of Bharatavarsha. The story converges on one kingdom and one hero rising to that seat, modelled on Krishna's role in the Mahabharata: not the strongest, but the one whose dharma includes the others.

## 4. Story-Layer Ground Rules (apply to all chapters)

These are inherited from `/Users/smk/repos/age-of-mythos/docs/CHARACTER_SYSTEM.md` and must hold across Chapters 2–4:

1. **Hero and villain share the same sacred animal.** The animal bond expresses differently — never make the villain bond with a *different* animal.
2. **Tattoo growth is earned, not gifted.** Chapter 2 = level 2→3 attempts (most fail). Chapter 3 = level 3 for surviving 6, level 4 for the Maha-Adhipati at the end of Chapter 4.
3. **Mahabharata parallels are shadows, never named in-fiction.** A character can echo Bhima or Karna — but the prose never says so.
4. **Villains must remain sympathetic.** Every Chapter 2 villain rise needs a moment where the reader agrees with them — usually grounded in a real grievance the kingdom failed to address in Chapter 1.
5. **The sacred bird is the omen and messenger.** In every chapter, the bird signals approaching shifts.
6. **Ancient weapon ≠ Modern weapon.** Ancient weapon for moments of dharmic truth; modern weapon for tactical/political work. Never equal.
7. **Regional culture is the texture.** Food, language, festivals, architecture must be specific. No pan-Indian generic backdrops.
8. **Six regions, one fate.** Each region has a flavour: South = memory & monument; North = throne & lineage; East = devotion & delta; West = self-rule & sea; Central = heartland & balance; Northeast = secret & spirit. Hold these flavours across all three chapters.

## 5. Cross-Chapter Throughlines (the spine that holds 84 stories together)

To prevent the saga from becoming 84 disconnected vignettes, six **throughlines** thread through every chapter. Every story in every chapter must touch at least one.

| # | Throughline | What it tracks |
|---|---|---|
| T1 | **The Void Maw** | An external cosmic threat foreshadowed since Chapter 1. It arrives properly in Chapter 4. Every chapter has 2–3 "Void Maw signs" planted across kingdoms. |
| T2 | **The Grand Council** | The mythical 7-seat body. Symbolic in Ch 1. Empty seats in Ch 2 (civil war prevents convening). 6 seats fill in Ch 3. 7th seat filled in Ch 4. |
| T3 | **The Bloodlines** | Heroes are linked through ancestral lineages (see `02-relationship-graph.md`). Many heroes share grandparents, gurus, or ancestor councils. This is the Mahabharata-family-tree spine. |
| T4 | **The Mirror Pairs** | Hero and villain of the same animal. The mirror tension escalates: in Ch 2 villains rise; in Ch 3 mirrors are forced into open confrontation; in Ch 4 the surviving mirrors unite or one consumes the other. |
| T5 | **The Animal Stirring** | The sacred animals themselves act as agents from Ch 2 onward — herds migrate, birds gather, beasts speak in dreams. By Ch 3 the animals choose their champions. By Ch 4 the animals fight visibly. |
| T6 | **The Tattoo Trial** | Each tattoo level requires a *specific kind* of suffering to earn. Ch 2 = the loss trial (you must lose something irreplaceable). Ch 3 = the unity trial (you must accept a former enemy). Ch 4 = the surrender trial (the Maha-Adhipati must release personal claim). |

## 6. Recommended Production Order (when stories get written)

Do **NOT** write Chapter 2 kingdom-by-kingdom in alphabetical order. Write by *region clusters* so cross-kingdom relationships develop in tandem:

1. **Karnataka cluster first** (Vijayanagara Reborn + immediate neighbours: TN, Kerala, AP) — the pilot. Establishes voice and pacing.
2. **Northeast cluster** (all 8 NE kingdoms together) — they share borders, tribes, and storms.
3. **North cluster** (5 north kingdoms) — the Ganga line and the Himalayas.
4. **West + Central cluster** (combine these for the trade corridor).
5. **East cluster** (4 east kingdoms — Kalinga, Bengal, Bihar, Jharkhand).

Within each cluster, write the **Regional Dominant first** (the one chosen for Chapter 3 rise), then the kingdoms that fall into its orbit. This guarantees the Chapter 3 → 4 spine is solid before subordinate stories are written.
