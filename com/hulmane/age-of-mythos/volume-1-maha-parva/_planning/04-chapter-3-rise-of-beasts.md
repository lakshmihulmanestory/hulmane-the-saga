## 1. The Spine of Chapter 3

Chapter 3 has two parallel arcs running through the same 12 months of story-time:

**Arc A — The Beast-Rising (cosmic).** The animals stir on their own. Migration patterns reverse. Predators stop hunting. Birds form sky-armies. Heroes who are *bonded properly* feel it first; heroes whose bonds frayed in Ch 2 are left behind.

**Arc B — The Regional Convergence (political).** Inside each region, the surviving kingdoms cannot stand alone. They merge — by alliance, conquest, or absorption — into a single Regional Dominant. Six dominants emerge, one per region. Each Dominant claims its regional seat at the Grand Council.

The two arcs converge at the chapter's climax: the **first convening of the Six**, held inside a sacred sanctuary the *animals choose* (not the heroes). Outside the sanctuary, the Void Maw watches.

## 2. The Six Regional Dominants (rationale + alternatives)

### Region 1 — South Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Vijayanagara Reborn (Karnataka) — Tungabhadra.**
- **Why:** Already the franchise pilot; Bloodline-A heart; Parashurama's chosen successor for the 109th form; the Indian Elephant is the largest, oldest sacred-animal awakening.
- **Absorbed: Kerala (Parashurama steps down, transfers the 108-form school under Tungabhadra's banner) | Andhra Pradesh (Krishnaveni reveals as cousin-sister; joins willingly) | Tamil Nadu (Kurinjiselvi joins as the South's voice in the council; not absorbed but federated under Karnataka) | Telangana (Chittaranga's mirror-city is split — half absorbed, half independent technical city-state).**
- **Alternative if user changes mind:** Kerala (Parashurama-Kshetra) is the only viable alternative.

### Region 2 — North Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Ganga-Simhasana (Uttar Pradesh) — but ruled by Gangaputra-as-protector, with young Yamunadatta as throne-figure.**
- **Why:** The Ganga throne is canonically the seat of Bharatavarsha's central political memory. Gangaputra (Arjuna parallel) is the natural North heart. The Krishna-shadow in him fulfils — he serves another's throne rather than holding his own.
- **Absorbed: Haryana (Kurukshetraa lives, joins as Yamunadatta's regent) | Madhya Pradesh (Dvadashashringa is Yamunadatta's actual uncle by Bloodline-B; pledges) | Punjab (Panchanada — equal alliance, NOT absorption — Punjab keeps sovereignty but defers militarily) | Uttarakhand (Kasturika joins; brings the sacred-pass tradition) | Himachal Pradesh (Himavati joins; brings the monastic order).**
- **Note:** This is the *largest* dominant by population and the trickiest politically — five kingdoms united, three resistant.
- **Alternative:** Haryana (Kurukshetraa) — but she's better as Yamunadatta's regent.

### Region 3 — East Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Kalinga-Chakra (Odisha) — Jagannathi.**
- **Why:** Jagannath is the only deity in Bharatavarsha that is *non-sectarian* — tribal, brahmanic, merchant. Once Jagannathi pulls the chariot in Ch 2, she becomes the only figure all three cults accept. The Yudhishthira parallel fits a Dominant who rules by being trusted.
- **Absorbed: Bihar (Vajramukha and Maitreyi the Younger marry; Bihar joins as the *brain* of the East dominant) | Jharkhand (Vanajara stays; defers; the coal question is unresolved going into Ch 4) | West Bengal (Sundarvani joins; her father's faction is broken; Kala-Nadi remains free — a live threat).**
- **Alternative:** West Bengal (Sundarvani) — but her family wound makes her too divided for regional rule.

### Region 4 — West Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Swarajya Sahyadri (Maharashtra) — Shivagati.**
- **Why:** She is the only Chapter-2 survivor who emerged with **two animal bonds** (squirrel + gaur from Goa). The unprecedented double-bond is the cosmic sign that she carries more than one kingdom's load. Hanuman-Abhimanyu archetype fits the West's self-rule ethos.
- **Absorbed: Goa (occupied throughout Ch 3; resistance led by Ferreira-de-Sangam, federated under Shivagati) | Gujarat (Simhavikrama joins after a near-loss against Pakistan-coded external pressure; the lion + squirrel pair becomes the regional emblem) | Rajasthan (Mrigatrishna joins as Shivagati's strategy advisor; the Shakuni-hero finds her Yudhishthira-partner).**
- **Alternative:** Gujarat (Simhavikrama). But Shivagati's double-bond is the cosmic signal.

### Region 5 — Central Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Hridaya-Sthana (Madhya Pradesh) — Dvadashashringa.**
- **Why:** Geographically central. Bloodline-B link to Gangaputra makes Central the *moderator* region between North and South. Dvadashashringa's Yudhishthira mind, freed from his 12-court paralysis in Ch 2, can finally rule.
- **Absorbed: Chhattisgarh (Dandakarni joins as the *forest-conscience* of Central; her tribal faction is recognized as a co-equal council-voice — a first in any region).**
- **Note:** Central is the *smallest* dominant by kingdom count (just 2). This is intentional — Central rules by balance, not by mass. Both Hridaya-Sthana and Dandakaranya retain near-full sovereignty inside a loose pact.
- **Alternative:** Chhattisgarh (Dandakarni). But the heartland-deer is the natural Yudhishthira seat.

### Region 6 — Northeast Bharatavarsha
**Dominant: Ganda-Kshetra (Assam) — Kamalavarna.**
- **Why:** She is the matriarch of Chandravamsha (Bloodline C). Once she stops fearing her power in Ch 2, she is the most cosmically powerful hero of all 28 — tantric Shakti has no equal. The rhino-bond is the rarest awakening.
- **Absorbed: Meghalaya (Meghanadi is Kamalavarna's younger sister; joins naturally) | Manipur (Moirangthem's wife is Chandravamsha; family ties bring Manipur in) | Nagaland (Dzulevira is niece; she joins reluctantly, retains tribal autonomy) | Sikkim (Kanchenjunga is cousin via Vana-Kula crossover; joins as the spiritual conscience) | Arunachal Pradesh (Udayagiri joins after the First Herald is dealt with) | Mizoram (under Hmangaihi, the niece of Tlangvala; joins) | Tripura (under Triputra; joins; the kingdom is rebuilding under his hand).**
- **Note:** This is the *most fragmented* dominant — 8 kingdoms with strong regional identities. Kamalavarna's tantric authority is the only force that can hold them. Chapter 3 NE is a test of *how matriarchy works at scale*.
- **Alternative:** Manipur (Moirangthem). But the Sangai-Nata kingdom is too small as a base.

## 3. The Universal Chapter-3 Structure (per Dominant)

Each Regional Dominant story uses this scaffold:

```
PHASE 1 — THE ANIMAL CHOOSES         The sacred animal of the would-be Dominant
                                     performs a public, undeniable act
                                     (a herd convergence, a bird-army, a beast
                                     speaking through a hero's tattoo).
                                     Other kingdoms in the region SEE this.

PHASE 2 — THE BARGAIN OR THE BATTLE  Each absorbed kingdom must decide:
                                     bargain (alliance) or battle (refusal).
                                     The bargain costs something (a child given,
                                     a relic surrendered, an ancestor renamed).
                                     The battle costs more.
                                     The Regional Dominant must do both at least once.

PHASE 3 — THE TATTOO-3 TRIAL         The Dominant attempts tattoo-3.
                                     The trial is the UNITY TRIAL — they must
                                     accept into their inner council a person
                                     they hated in Ch 2 or Ch 1.
                                     Success = half-body tattoo earned.
                                     Failure = the Dominant survives but the seat
                                     is held by a regent (rare; not in our spine).

EPILOGUE — THE CONVENING             The Dominant rides to the Council Sanctuary.
                                     The Animal flies/walks ahead of them.
                                     The Void Maw watches from outside the sanctuary
                                     and is heard for the first time.
```

## 7. The Void Maw in Chapter 3 — From Sign to Sound

Where Ch 2 had silent signs, Ch 3 has **audible Maw-events**:

- The Maw is **heard** through the dreams of every hero. A low chord — not a voice, not a word. They wake together, on the same night, across all six regions.
- The Maw's **three Heralds** (planted in Ch 2) **walk together** for the first time in the third act. They are seen at Hampi by Tungabhadra and Hampi-of-the-Bronze. The child sees them clearly; Tungabhadra sees only the absence where they should be.
- A **fourth Herald** appears at the end of Ch 3, after the Sanctuary convening — emerging from the Maw itself. This Herald has a name, and it is one of the names of a Ch-1 villain who was thought dead. (Open question: which one? Recommend **Krodha** of Sikkim — he was the most "compassion-aborbed" villain and the most likely to come back as a Maw-instrument.)

## 8. The Sanctuary Convening — Climax of Chapter 3

The location: **the buried temple at Hampi** (Karnataka). Bhoomi-Takshaka's network of serpent-tunnels turns out to lead to a chamber the kingdoms didn't know existed. Tungabhadra opens it because her tattoo-3 is required to open it.

Inside: a stone slab with **seven seats carved into it**, in a circle. Six are clean. The seventh is blackened.

The Six Dominants sit. Six animals sit behind them. The Void Maw speaks for the first time — not in words but in the floor: the seventh seat *cracks* in real time, splitting from the slab.

The chapter ends with the Six standing up, leaving the chamber, and Tungabhadra placing her hand on the cracked seat. The seat does not crack further. The cosmic stalemate of Chapter 4 has begun.
