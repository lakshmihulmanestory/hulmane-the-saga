# Cross-Kingdom Relationship Graph & Family Trees

> The Mahabharata's power is not in its battles — it is in the fact that everyone in it is *related*.
> Cousins fight cousins. Gurus teach both sides. Mothers shelter rivals.
> This file builds that web for the kingdoms before any Chapter 2 story is written.

## 1. The Five Great Bloodlines

To avoid 28 unrelated heroes, we organize them into **five ancestral bloodlines** descended from mythic founders. Every hero (and many villains) belongs to exactly one bloodline. Bloodlines are NOT introduced in Chapter 1 prose — they emerge in Chapter 2 as ancestral claims surface during the civil wars.

### Bloodline A — **The Gajavamsha (Elephant Lineage)** — South + East
Descended from the elephant-rider founders of the buried temple-cities. Their crest: the Gaja-Mukut (elephant crown).

| Hero / Villain | Kingdom | Relation in lineage |
|---|---|---|
| Tungabhadra (H) | Karnataka | Direct heir — Maha-Empress of the line |
| Bhoomi-Takshaka (V) | Karnataka | Cousin-sister, dispossessed branch |
| Parashurama (H) | Kerala | Granduncle (the line's oldest living elder) |
| Kala-Hasta (V) | Kerala | Parashurama's exiled disciple, *not* blood but inherits the legacy |
| Vanajara (H) | Jharkhand | Distant cousin via the Eastern Ghats migration |
| Krishnaveni (H) | Andhra Pradesh | Half-sister to Tungabhadra (different mothers — see node `M-Ganga`) |
| Kalaniksha (V) | Andhra Pradesh | Krishnaveni's twin (lost at birth, returned hostile) |

Founder myth: A clan that rode the war elephants of the original Vijayanagara, scattered when the empire fell, but kept the seismic-bond intact across exile.

### Bloodline B — **The Suryavamsha (Solar Line)** — North + Central
Descended from the Surya-king of Hastinapura. The classic Mahabharata-throne line.

| Hero / Villain | Kingdom | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| Gangaputra (H) | Uttar Pradesh | The Suryavamsha throne-heir (the Arjuna analogue) |
| Adharma (V) | Uttar Pradesh | Half-brother (Karna-shadow — born first, raised wrong) |
| Kurukshetraa (H) | Haryana | First cousin — daughter of Gangaputra's father's brother |
| Kuru-Bhasma (V) | Haryana | Kurukshetraa's husband-turned-rival (the bitter marriage) |
| Dvadashashringa (H) | Madhya Pradesh | Younger half-brother of Gangaputra (the Yudhishthira-mind to Gangaputra's Arjuna-arm) |
| Adharma-Kendra (V) | Madhya Pradesh | Maternal uncle to Gangaputra and Dvadashashringa (the Shakuni position) |
| Panchanada (H) | Punjab | Cousin via the Kuru-Panchala marriage |
| Panchavish (V) | Punjab | Panchanada's disinherited brother |

Founder myth: One king who ruled all of Aryavarta, whose seven sons each took a piece. The fragments fight to remember the whole.

### Bloodline C — **The Chandravamsha (Lunar Line)** — Northeast + East upper
Descended from the moon-king of Pragjyotisha. Tantric, secret, matrilineal.

| Hero / Villain | Kingdom | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| Kamalavarna (H) | Assam | Matriarch of the line (the line is matrilineal) |
| Kama-Mara (V) | Assam | Kamalavarna's mother's sister's daughter — first-cousin and dark-cult inheritor |
| Meghanadi (H) | Meghalaya | Younger sister to Kamalavarna |
| Megha-Daitya (V) | Meghalaya | The line's only male claimant — pushed out by matrilineal rule |
| Dzulevira (H) | Nagaland | Niece (Kamalavarna's elder sister's daughter) |
| Naga-Rakshasa (V) | Nagaland | A serpent-bound man Kamalavarna's mother once spared |
| Sundarvani (H) | West Bengal | Distant cousin via the Brahmaputra-Ganga delta |
| Kala-Nadi (V) | West Bengal | Sundarvani's own twin (split at birth in the delta) |
| Sangaputra-line | Manipur | (the Sangai is the line's totem; Moirangthem is a son of this house) |
| Moirangthem (H) | Manipur | Cousin |
| Tamasa-Nrita (V) | Manipur | Moirangthem's dance-guru who turned |

Founder myth: A moon-queen who could not be killed, who scattered her daughters across the mist-mountains so the line could never be cut in one strike.

### Bloodline D — **The Vana-Kula (Forest Clan)** — Central + East lower + Northeast lower
Descended from the forest-mothers of the Dandakaranya — older than empire.

| Hero / Villain | Kingdom | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| Dandakarni (H) | Chhattisgarh | The line's living head — eldest of the wild |
| Dandaka-Rakshasa (V) | Chhattisgarh | Dandakarni's twin brother — the city-pulled half |
| Vanajara (H) | Jharkhand | Younger cousin — but also cross-bound to Bloodline A |
| Vana-Raksha (V) | Jharkhand | Vanajara's adoptive elder (clan-uncle, not blood) |
| Kanchenjunga (H) | Sikkim | Cousin via the eastern forest migration |
| Krodha (V) | Sikkim | Was a clan-brother — exiled for the first rage |
| Tlangvala (H) | Mizoram | Forest cousin — the line's southern remnant |
| Parvata-Asura (V) | Mizoram | Outside the line — a mountain that adopted the clan name to claim them |
| Udayagiri (H) | Arunachal | Cousin via the rising-sun migration north |
| Asta-Asura (V) | Arunachal | Udayagiri's own shadow split off at first dawn (mystic-twin) |
| Tripurari (H) | Tripura | Cousin via the three-cities founding |
| Tripura-Asura (V) | Tripura | Was a stone-spirit gifted by Tripurari's grandfather — turned |

Founder myth: A mother-forest that walked. Her footsteps became kingdoms; her shadow became villains.

### Bloodline E — **The Sagaravamsha (Ocean & Stone)** — West + South coastal
Descended from the ocean-king who married the desert-queen. The reconcilers of opposites.

| Hero / Villain | Kingdom | Relation |
|---|---|---|
| Sagaradeva (H) | Goa | Direct heir |
| Maha-Vega (V) | Goa | Sagaradeva's storm-cursed half-brother |
| Simhavikrama (H) | Gujarat | First cousin — the desert-edge of the line |
| Kala-Simha (V) | Gujarat | Simhavikrama's nephew (his elder sister's son) claiming primogeniture |
| Shivagati (F, H) | Maharashtra | Mountain-side niece of Sagaradeva |
| Vanaraksha (V) | Maharashtra | Shivagati's literal elder brother (already canonical) |
| Mrigatrishna (H) | Rajasthan | Cousin via the desert-queen's line |
| Maru-Yama (V) | Rajasthan | The desert-queen's ghost-servant who outlived her |

Founder myth: Where the sea met the desert, a king and queen built a bridge of stone. Their children inherit both the salt and the sand.

## 2. Cross-Bloodline Marriages (The Mahabharata Trick)

The five bloodlines are not isolated — they are **woven by marriage**. These named unions create the most explosive Chapter 2/3 loyalty conflicts.

| Union | Bloodline cross | What it produces in Ch 2–4 |
|---|---|---|
| Gangaputra's mother was Gajavamsha-born | B + A | Gangaputra (UP) and Tungabhadra (Karnataka) are *first cousins*. Becomes a Ch 4 unification fulcrum. |
| Kurukshetraa's grandmother was Vana-Kula | B + D | She has a forest-mother whose voice returns in her tattoo-3 trial. |
| Sundarvani married into Bloodline B via her father | C + B | Her child (introduced in Ch 2) is the Adharma-shadow's nephew — explains why Bengal can't stay neutral. |
| Shivagati's mother was Chandravamsha | E + C | She is Kamalavarna's first cousin once removed. Their alliance in Ch 3 is blood, not strategy. |
| Parashurama trained both Tungabhadra and Kala-Hasta | A (internal) | The Drona/Karna fracture lives inside Kerala. |
| Krishnaveni and Kalaniksha as lost twins | A (internal) | Andhra Pradesh is *one woman split* — the kingdom is the body. |
| Dandakarni and Dandaka-Rakshasa as twin siblings | D (internal) | Chhattisgarh's civil war is literally siblings. |
| Moirangthem's wife is Dzulevira's elder sister | C (internal) | Manipur and Nagaland share a child by Ch 3. |

## 3. The Council of Gurus

There are seven living gurus across Bharatavarsha. Each teaches multiple heroes. Their loyalties — and their secrets — drive much of Chapter 2.

| Guru | Tradition | Teaches | Hidden truth (revealed Ch 2–3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sage Vidyaranya** (spirit) | Vijayanagara philosophy | Tungabhadra; also visited Krishnaveni once | Was once mortal-married to Bhoomi-Takshaka's grandmother. He carries guilt. |
| **Mahaguru Drona-of-the-Plain** | Kuru warrior code | Gangaputra, Kurukshetraa, Panchanada | His true loyalty is to Adharma-Kendra (his maternal uncle's debt). |
| **The Silent Mother** (a clouded leopard who speaks in dreams) | Animal-bond mysticism | Kamalavarna, Meghanadi, Dzulevira | She is the line's founder herself, undying. |
| **Old Parashurama** (himself, as guru) | The 108 forms | Trains: every Bloodline A hero who comes to him; refused Kala-Hasta | Refused to teach the 109th not from honor but from fear — he discovered it and could not unlearn it. |
| **Sage Agastya** (river-bound) | Sangam knowledge | Kurinjiselvi, Sagaradeva, Mrigatrishna (briefly) | Knows the location of all five surviving Sangam texts; one of them names the Void Maw. |
| **Bodhi Lama** | Beyul compassion tradition | Kanchenjunga, Himavati, Tlangvala | He was once Krodha's first companion before exile — could have stopped him and didn't. |
| **Aranya-Mata** (forest mother) | Vana-Kula clan rites | Dandakarni, Vanajara, Udayagiri | She knows Bloodline D's "stolen members" — heroes who are Vana-Kula but don't know it. |

When a guru speaks across kingdoms (Ch 2), the kingdoms have to reckon with the fact that their elders share secrets the heroes don't.

## 4. The Alliance Web (Pre-Civil-War, end of Ch 1)

Even before the civil wars, some kingdoms had visible *historical* alliances and rivalries. These are the cracks Chapter 2 widens.

### Standing alliances (formal, signed)
- **The Southern Triad:** Karnataka — Tamil Nadu — Andhra Pradesh. Trade pact, shared river-rights, sacred-elephant exchange.
- **The River Sisters:** West Bengal — Bihar — UP. Ganga water-rights pact.
- **The Mountain Pact:** Uttarakhand — Himachal Pradesh — Sikkim. Pilgrimage and snow-monastery confederation.
- **The Five-Forest Accord:** Chhattisgarh — Jharkhand — Odisha — Madhya Pradesh — Maharashtra inland. Tribal-rights & forest protection.
- **The Naga Council:** Nagaland — Manipur — Arunachal Pradesh. Naga-deer-mithun sacred-animal triple.

### Standing rivalries (cold)
- Karnataka ↔ Kerala (the 108-form schism, generations old).
- Maharashtra ↔ Gujarat (the trade-vs-tradition fight, since the Lion's Gate accord broke).
- UP ↔ Bihar (the Magadha-Kosala memory — both claim the eldest throne).
- Tamil Nadu ↔ Andhra Pradesh (the Sangam-river dispute).
- Tripura ↔ Assam (the Brahmaputra delta sovereignty).

### Open hostilities (sporadic raids, no formal war)
- Punjab ↔ Haryana (Kuru-Panchala unresolved).
- Meghalaya ↔ Assam (the cloud vs the rhino).
- Mizoram ↔ Tripura (forest-mountain border).
- West Bengal ↔ Odisha (Bay of Bengal claim).

Chapter 2 collapses **every cold rivalry into hot war** and **every alliance into betrayal-or-renewal stress.**

## 6. The Seven Faces of the Grand Council (Ch 4 target seats)

The Grand Council has seven seats, six regional + one supreme. The plan is:

| Seat | Region | Filled by (proposed) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | South | Tungabhadra |
| 2 | North | Gangaputra |
| 3 | East | Jagannathi |
| 4 | West | Shivagati |
| 5 | Central | Dvadashashringa |
| 6 | Northeast | Kamalavarna |
| 7 | Maha-Adhipati (supreme) | Tungabhadra (also holds her regional seat — or her seat passes to Kerala's surviving line) |

Note: the Bloodline ties mean these seven are not strangers — Tungabhadra and Gangaputra are first cousins. Kamalavarna and Shivagati are also cousins (C+E). So Chapter 4's "unification" is partly a family reunion under duress.

These are NEW characters not in Chapter 1's `heroes.csv` / `villains.csv`. They must be added when their kingdom's story is written.

- **Hampi-of-the-Bronze** — Tungabhadra's young ward (orphan from the Buried Temple). Becomes her Chapter 3 conscience.

### Council Elders & Antagonists (introduced Ch 2)
- **Vyasa-of-the-Margins** — a chronicler-monk who appears in every region, recording the civil wars. He is older than any character. He has no kingdom — he serves the Grand Council seat 7 in absentia. (Ch 4 reveals he is the Maha-Adhipati's witness, not a candidate.)
- **The Six Regional Marshals** — military commanders, one per region, who outlive Ch 2 but die in Ch 3. They are sacrificial — their deaths buy each Regional Dominant the right to claim the regional seat.

### Void Maw Heralds (introduced Ch 2, escalating to Ch 4)
- **The First Herald** — appears in Northeast first (an Arunachal mystic). A man with no shadow.
- **The Second Herald** — appears in West (a Goa sailor returned without a ship).
- **The Third Herald** — appears in South (a Karnataka child born during a temple-earthquake).

The three heralds converge in Chapter 4. They are not villains — they are *warnings*. Heroes who kill them in Chapter 2 unwittingly accelerate the Void Maw arrival.

## 9. Rules for Using This Graph

1. **Don't reveal bloodlines in Chapter 1 retrofits.** Chapter 1 is canon. Bloodlines surface only in Chapter 2 onward via ancestral visions, family-shrine inscriptions, or council scrolls.
2. **A villain's bloodline grants sympathy.** When the villain claims a blood-tie, the hero must hesitate.
3. **A guru's secret is a Chekhov's gun.** Once written into a story, the secret must explode by Chapter 4.
4. **No "lost prince returns" cliché.** When lost-twins like Krishnaveni/Kalaniksha exist, the lost half is already known to the kingdom — the kingdom just refused to remember.
5. **Maintain Mahabharata fractality.** Every alliance must contain at least one cousin on each side.
