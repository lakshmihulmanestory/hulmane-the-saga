The Saga Prologue

History remembers the Mahabharata as the war of brothers, fought for the soil of a kingdom. But the soil has turned to dust, and the kingdom has expanded to the stars.

Today, the world of nations is fractured. The old kings have traded their crowns for codes, and their chariots for satellites. They claim leadership by the virtue of their names, hiding behind the shadows of their ancestors. But as the borders of Earth bleed and the gears of global peace grind to a halt, a new cry rises from the chaos.

The war of the modern age is not for land — it is for the Truth.

The throne of the world — the ‘Hulmane’ — is a crown of thorns that rejects the unworthy. It does not care for bloodlines or hierarchies. It waits for the one whose mind is aligned with the universe. For in this saga, power is not a birthright; it is a burden earned through excellence.

As nations prepare for the final conflict, the ancient law is reborn in a new tongue:

“Where there is Merit, there is Truth.
And only in that Truth, can the Galaxy find its Victory.”

The Saga of Hulmane begins now.
